01 — The before

GREW UP WHERE dreaming big GOT YOU CUT DOWN.

I grew up in a boring city in North Africa, in a home where big dreams were met with sarcasm and doubt. Tall poppy syndrome was everywhere. If you dared to grow taller than those around you, they'd cut you down before you bloomed.

My parents loved me, but they were loud with limiting beliefs that were never mine to carry. Old school. No internet money. No online future.

Since I was young, something in my mind was always drawing maps. Planning, connecting, building systems in my head I didn't yet have the tools to put on paper.

I've never been able to explain something with just words and gestures. There's always a friction — a pull — to find a pen and paper first. It all needs to be drawn before it makes sense to me. That's not a habit. That's how my mind works.

02 — The factory

3AM. HEAVY WRENCHES. MASK ON.
PATCHES SPREADING.

At 21, I was lifting heavy wrenches at 3am inside old, disgusting machines in a metal factory. Sweating. Stinking. Wearing a mask 8 hours a day just to breathe through the dust and noise.

I have vitiligo — and every scratch from a piece of steel left a white patch on my skin. Every time stress spiked, the patches spread wider. My body was literally keeping score of that environment.

It was telling me what my mind already knew: this place is not for you.

"You don't belong here. You could do something impressive. Don't end up like us."

Factory workers aged 28–42 — trapped for years

Men who had been there 10, 15, 20 years — some smoking on every break just to survive — were telling me to run. That image never left me.

04 — The belief & the now

"The version of me I dreamed about wasn't far away. It was close. And it was coming."

The quiet belief I kept alive through everything

The turning point wasn't a single moment. It was an accumulation of hundreds of days of watching, studying, mapping, thinking. I discovered digital nomads who built from zero — from physical labor, immigrant backgrounds, living with family — and ended up running businesses from anywhere in the world.

Now I'm 24. Printing factory. Living with parents. Building in silence. Three months free from pornography. 3,500 TikTok followers. A hard drive full of mindmaps, systems, flowcharts and frameworks built late at night while the rest of the house was asleep.

"I studied all those years and I can't do what you do. People need this."

05 — Core beliefs

WHAT I KNOW TO BE true.

01

Identity

Identity is not fixed. It is an accumulation of small consistent circuits — every thought repeated, every action taken, compounding invisibly until one day you are unrecognizable to your old environment.

02

Escape

To escape a bad environment, become a ninja first. Move in silence. Quiet quit. Build your exit in the dark. Don't fight the tall poppy syndrome — just leave it behind. You will be alone in that process. That's the price of the exit.

03

Addiction

Anything shiny, free, instantly pleasurable and easy to reach is a trap. Run. Don't look at it. Don't smell it. Don't touch it. That's a trap George.

04

Systems thinking

Most people feel their way through life. The ones who escape fastest are the ones who can draw it — map the invisible, structure the chaos, see the system behind the struggle. You don't need a degree. You need the courage to open a blank canvas.
