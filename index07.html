01 — WHERE IT STARTED

The Wrong City

early days

I grew up in a boring city in North Africa, in a home where big dreams were met with sarcasm and doubt.

If you dared to grow taller than those around you, they would cut you down before you had the chance to bloom.

My parents loved me. But they were loud with limiting beliefs that were never mine to carry.

Since I was young I was already thinking differently. I would tell my mother I wanted to build businesses, invest, grow something. While other kids stayed comfortable, something in my mind was always drawing maps, planning, connecting, building systems in my head that I didn't yet have the tools to put on paper.

I have never been able to explain something just with words and hand gestures. There is always a friction, a pull, to find a pen and a piece of paper first. A calculation, a random topic, a life decision. It all needs to be drawn before it makes sense to me.

That is not a habit.
That is how my mind works.
02 — THE FACTORY

3am, Heavy Wrenches

the factory

At 21, I was lifting heavy wrenches at 3am inside old, disgusting machines in a metal factory.

Sweating. Stinking. Wearing a mask 8 hours a day just to breathe through the dust and noise.

I have vitiligo, and every scratch from a piece of steel left a white patch on my skin. Every time stress spiked, the patches spread wider. My body was literally keeping score of that environment.

It was telling me what my mind already knew. This place is not for you.
03 — THROWING EVERYTHING AT THE WALL

Looking for the Slingshot

During those years I tried everything to find my exit.

I launched a logo design service. I traded XAUUSD, EUR/USD, Bitcoin, S&P500 and lost every dirham I put in. I built two YouTube beatmaking channels and hit 1,500 followers, making real money from type beats. I registered an LLC for a solar panel agency, had the whole model mapped out, never landed the first client. I studied German to maybe escape to Europe. I explored breakdancing, calisthenics, running, hiking, music production, graphic design, marketing.

I was throwing everything at the wall, looking for the slingshot. Something to stretch the rubber back as far as possible, then launch me.

I found a mentor who opened the entire world of business to me. A man who looked at a mindmap I had built on Miro and said slowly:

"...okay. Did you make that yourself?"

I said yes, like it was nothing. He paused. That pause said everything.
04 — COLLAPSING ON THE INSIDE

Falling Apart

But I was also falling apart.

Coming home from factory shifts physically destroyed, then trying to build a business on Zoom calls simultaneously. Pornography addiction draining me mentally and spiritually. Too much caffeine. Going to sleep not to rest but to escape the guilt of giving up. The exhaustion, the noise at home, the stress spreading across my skin. Everything piling on at once.

And yet, even in the darkest moments, I kept one quiet, almost irrational belief alive. The version of me I dreamed about was not far away. It was close. And it was coming.
05 — THE MEN WHO TOLD ME TO RUN

Don't End Up Like Us

the shift

The older workers at the factory, men aged 28, 35, 42, would pull me aside and say:

"You don't belong here. You could do something impressive. Don't end up like us."

Men who had been trapped for years, some smoking on every break just to survive their shift, were telling me to run.

That image never left me.
06 — THE TURNING POINT

Hundreds of Days

The turning point was not a single moment. It was an accumulation of hundreds of days of watching, studying, mapping, thinking.

I discovered digital nomads who built from zero, from physical labor, from immigrant backgrounds, from living with family, and ended up shooting content and running businesses from anywhere in the world.

I did not just think that is cool.
I thought: that is possible.
And I am going to do that.
07 — WHERE I AM NOW

Building in Silence

right now

I am 24. Working in a printing factory. Living with my parents. Building in silence.

Three months free from pornography. 3,500 followers on TikTok. A hard drive full of mindmaps, systems, flowcharts and frameworks built late at night while the rest of the house was asleep.

My friend, a master's graduate in economics, looked at my maps recently and said:

"I studied all those years and I can't do what you do. People need this."

I am not a guru with a course and a Lamborghini. I am the guy still inside the escape, one step ahead of where you are, drawing the map in real time and showing you exactly how it works.
